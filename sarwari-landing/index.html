The Problem

Why Sawari?

Millions of commuters in Kathmandu Valley have no reliable way to navigate public transport. There is no official route map, no fare calculator, no way to know when a bus is coming.

No Route Information

Google Maps shows driving paths, not actual bus routes. Nepal's informal transit system is invisible online.

Expensive Alternatives

Ride-booking apps like Pathao cost 5–10× the bus fare. Not everyone can afford them daily.

Ask a Stranger

The most common method — unreliable, time-consuming, and impossible for tourists.

No Live Tracking

You stand at a stop with zero visibility. Is the bus 2 minutes away or 30?

Three steps from confusion to confident commuting.

The Impact

Why Is Sawari Needed?

Nepal's public transport carries millions daily, but lacks any digital infrastructure. Sawari fills that gap entirely.

Millions of Commuters, Zero Digital Tools

Kathmandu Valley alone has 4+ million people who rely on buses, microbuses, and tempos daily. There is no app, no website, no centralized database of routes. Sawari is the first system that maps, tracks, and routes Nepal's entire public transit network — built from the ground up with real hardware on real buses.

Tourists Left Stranded

Visitors can't read Nepali signs or ask conductors in the local language. Sawari provides exact boarding phrases, fare estimates, and visual route maps — making public transit accessible to everyone regardless of language.

Environmental Necessity

Every ride diverted from a private car to a public bus saves 0.121 kg of CO₂ per kilometer. With Sawari making buses easier to use, we reduce emissions while saving commuters money.

57% less CO₂ per km vs. cars

Safety & Fair Pricing

Conductors sometimes overcharge tourists or unfamiliar riders. Sawari shows the government-regulated fare before you board — including student and elderly discounts, rounded to the nearest NPR 5 as per Nepal's transport rules.
